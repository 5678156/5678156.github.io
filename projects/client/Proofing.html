Fermenting/Proofing
  • Once you have mixed the dough, the sourdough starter will start to ferment the dough, which will give it its nice rise and airy feel.
  • There are 2 phases to Fermenting.
    1. The first phase is called Bulk Fermentation.
      • During Bulk Fermentation, the dough will become less sticky and more cohesive. It will be easier to handle.
      • For the first 2 hours, you must perform a series of "stretch and folds" every 30 minutes.
      • See photos but you simply lift a side of the dough and fold it over the top.
      • Turn the bowl 90 degrees and do another stretch and fold.
      • Do it a total of 4 times.
      • Then cover and let sit for 30 more minutes.
      • You will do stretch and folds 4 times during the first 2 hours.
      • Then you let the dough continue to bulk ferment on the counter for another 2-3 hours until it has doubled in size and looks airy.
    2. During the Cold Proofing, you dump the dough out of the mixing bowl and do a series of folds and then flip the dough over so the seam side is down.
      • Use your fingers to turn and pull the dough so the seam side down becomes tighter and the top is "jiggly" and smooth.
      • Now turn the dough upside down into a floured basket.
      • The seams will now be on top and the smooth side in the bottom of the floured basket.
      • Cover and put into the fridge for 6-12 hours or overnight.
      • The dough will continue to ferment slowly in the fridge in the shape you made and expand a bit into the shape of the basket.
      • Basket can be round or oval.
